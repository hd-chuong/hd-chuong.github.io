Having fresh air is an inviolable right.

However,

98%

of citizens in developing nations suffer from poor air quality.

3.7

million of deaths are caused by air pollution each year around the world.

One of the most polluting cities in the world is Beijing.

This is a visualisation of Beijing air quality

The air quality index is measured by the maximum index of six major air pollutants, including:

PM10

particles with a diameter of 10 micrometres or less, small enough to get through throats, noses and lungs

PM2.5

particles with a diameter of 2.5 micrometres or less, small enough to get through lungs into bloodstreams.

SO2

causes coughing, wheezing, shortness of breath, or a chest tightness.

NO2

inflames the lining of the lungs, and it can reduce immunity to lung infections.

O3

causes pain in ears, eyes, nose and throat and coughing.

CO

in large amount prevents brains, nerve tissues and heart from receiving enough oxygen to work properly.
